iOS For Beginners


Intro – Setting Up The Environment

30 minute lesson

Objective

Given access to this lesson, students will be able to set up the necessary environment for iOS programming by downloading the necessary tools and registering for the Apple Developer Program.



Getting The Tools

To get started on developing iOS applications, you'll need a few things.

  • A Macbook
  • An Apple ID
  • The Xcode Application
  • An Apple Developer Certification


If you have a Macbook, you must have an Apple ID that is used to access the Apple Store.

Thus, we will first focus on downloading Apple's own Integrated Development Environment (IDE) called Xcode, which will allow us to create our own mobile applications.



Downloading XCode


  1. Go to the App Store from your Macbook’s dock.



  2. In the App Store, search for Xcode.



  3. Download the Xcode application. You will need to sign in using your Apple ID and password. You will also require 15 GB or more of free space on your drive.



    4. We’ve installed XCode! We will learn how to get stared on using it in the next tutorial, but first we must check our Apple Developer Program status.



Licensing The Apple Developer Program

Apple requires all developers to join the Apple Developer Program in order to publish applications. This costs $99 a year.

Note: Recently, Apple has made it possible for developers to develop apps and test on their own devices without a license. If you’re uncertain about continuing development after this course, skip ahead to the next tutorial.



To join this program:

  1. Visit this link.

  2. Select "Start Your Enrollment" at the bottom.



  3. Sign into the Apple Developer Portal using your Apple ID and password.



  4. Once you sign in, redo Step 1 and Step 2.

  5. This time, you will be given terms and conditions to sign. Read and submit your agreement.



  6. In the following screen, choose Individual as your entity type.



  7. In true Apple fashion, you should be given another terms and conditions with fields to fill out once more. Press continue when you’re done.



  8. Confirm your details in the following screen and continue.



  9. Proceed to the payment screen, enter your information, and purchase your Apple Developer membership!



Congratulations, you are now a certified Apple Developer Program member! You can now use this link to visit membership controls, view your apps, view your privileges, certificates, and so on.




Summary

You now have Apple’s integrated development environment, Xcode, and you are a registered Apple Developer Program member. This will give you access to develop and publish applications on the iPhone, iPad, and iMac.

You also have access to the online portal through which you can manage your account, which will be instrumental to your functions as you continue your learning as an iOS developer.

With the setup out of the way, we can now move on to the next tutorial where you will get familiarized with the Xcode atmosphere, code a simple app, and run it on your own device!
